China's history spans over 5,000 years, beginning with early settlements along the Yellow River and developing through a succession of dynasties, including the Shang, Zhou, Han, Tang, and Qing. China's dynasties include the Xia (legendary), Shang, Zhou, Qin, Han, Jin, Sui, Tang, Song, Yuan, Ming, and Qing, among many others.

The longest river in China and the third-longest river in the world, the 6,300 kilometer Yangtze River starts at the southern foothills of the snow-covered Geladandong Peak of the Tanggula Mountains in southwestern Qinghai Province. We Chinese call the Yangtze River, Chang-jiang, which means “Long River”, or just “Jiang” — river. It flows past 2 autonomous regions and 6 provinces including Qinghai, Tibet, Sichuan, Yunnan, Hubei, Hunan, Jiangxi, Anhui, Jiangsu, and Shanghai City, where it runs into the East China Sea.
