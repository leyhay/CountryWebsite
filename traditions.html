The traditions of China are Chinese New Year Celebrations like the Mid-Autumn Festival, Dragon Boat Festival and Lantern Festival. China has 23 provinces and the People's Republic of China (PRC) currently administers 22 of them. Each province has their own cultural identity. They are each celebrated in unique ways that make them more appreciated.

Dragon boat racing is a tradition celebrated in China to commemorate the life and patriotic suicide of the ancient Chinese poet Qu Yuan, with traditions like dragon boat racing (symbolizing villagers searching for him) and eating zongzi. It's a vibrant water sport from ancient China, featuring long, dragon-decorated boats paddled by synchronized teams, led by a drummer setting the rhythm and a steerer guiding the boat,

Lantern festival is a vibrant Asian celebration marking the first full moon of the lunar year, ending Chinese New Year. It has colorful lanterns, riddles, dragon dances, and tangyuan to symbolize peace, family, and good fortune. The 15th day of the first lunar month, signaling the end of Spring Festival.

Qing ming or tomb sweeping day is known for honoring ancestors through grave cleaning and offerings like food, tea, and burning spirit money. It translates to Pure Brightness Festival, takes place on the fifteenth day after the spring equinox. As a holiday devoted to remembering and honoring the deceased, people sweep and decorate gravesites, burn incense.

Zhong qiu jie or mid autumn festival is a major Chinese harvest festival, celebrated on the 15th day of the 8th lunar month (around Sept/Oct), symbolizing family reunion, harvest, and gratitude for the full moon, featuring traditions like eating mooncakes, lighting lanterns, and telling stories, most famously the legend of the moon goddess Chang'e. It's observed by Chinese communities globally and in other parts of Asia like Vietnam, Korea, and Japan.

Chinese new year is a celebration of the arrival of spring and the beginning of a new year on the lunisolar calendar. It is the most important holiday in China, and it is also widely celebrated in South Korea, Vietnam, and countries with a significant overseas Chinese population. Do a big house clean before the day, decorate with red & gold, wear new red clothes, have a big family reunion dinner with symbolic foods (like fish for surplus!), give red envelopes

Caligraphy is an ancient, meditative art form using brush, ink, and paper, also known as the fpur treasures, to create expressive characters, focusing on stroke technique, structure, and spirit, with major styles like Seal, Clerical, Standard, Running, and Cursive, evolving from pictographs into sophisticated visual art central to Chinese culture and self-expression, similar to meditation.

Red envelopes are symbolic packets, usually red and gold, holding money, given during Lunar New Year and other celebrations like weddings and birthdays to bestow good fortune, happiness, and prosperity, warding off evil spirits. They signify goodwill, respect, and blessings, especially from elders to younger, symbolizing wishes for a prosperous year. It's meant to pass down the luck and fortune.

Peking Opera is a highly stylized Chinese performance art combining singing, recitation, acting, and martial arts. The elaborate costumes, symbolic makeup, and distinct character roles represent different social types.
