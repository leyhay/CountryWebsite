China's Food

The typical foods that originate from China have unique and savory flavors. All of the foods in China each have their unforgettable tastes and aspects that make them stand out from the rest of the dishes. Their taste has rich flavor that makes you reminice back to your childhood.

Xiao long bao (小笼包)is little soup dumplings that are famous in China and overseas. They are eaten at family gatherings, a celebration of some sorts, or even eating them just because they're so good. The filling choice can vary but the most common filling is pork.

Hot pot(火锅) is world known meal. It has good bases that you can choose and put food items into the pot to cook. It's a popular East Asian cuisine where food is cooked in a pot of flavorful broth at the center of the table.

Char siu (叉烧), also known as barbecued pork is a very famous dish known for its flavor. It's a popular Cantonese barbecued pork known for its sweet, savory flavor, reddish color, and glaze. The strong flavor can be eaten alone, served with rice, in noodle dishes, or as a filling for buns like cha siu bao.

Peking duck (北京烤鸭) is duck with great flavors that can be eaten alone or paired with bread and cucumbers. The preparation involves air-drying the duck for up to 48 hours after being glazed, and it's often served in multiple courses.

Tomato and egg (番茄炒蛋) is a dish known for the savory flavors the egg and tomato provide. They complement each others flavors that makes it a beloved dish.

Tang yuan (汤圆) is glutinous rice balls with sweet or savory fillings. They are mainly eaten on Winter Solstice. They can be plain or filled with black sesame, red bean, peanut, or savory ingredients.

Zongzi (粽子) is rice wrapped in bamboo sheets with sweet or savory fillings. They are mainly eaten during the dragon boat festival. They can be filled with pork, mushrooms, salted egg yolk, or red bean paste. The leaves adds a unique flavor, and the cooking process lets everything become combined.

Moon cakes (月饼) are eaten during Mid autumn festival to celebrate family reunion. Its fillings can be lotus seed, red bean paste, but it's often salted duck egg yolks. They are given as gifts symbolizing good fortune.

Sticky rice (糯米饭), is glutinous rice with savory flavors that can be paired with Do fu hua. Sticky rice can be eaten on any occasion. It has savory ingredients like salted cashews, shitake mushrooms, and cheong, and is garnished with fresh green onions for an added pop of flavor. It's a filling and satisfying meal that makes it perfect to eat anytime.

Tofu pudding ((豆腐花) is tofu with soy milk to bring out the sweet flavor. It is commonly know to be paired with no mi fan because their flavors match each other. It is a or savory dessert made from very soft soy curds and can be toppe off with sweet syrups or savory toppings.
